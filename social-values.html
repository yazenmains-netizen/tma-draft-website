Our Commitment

More Than Maintenance.
A Commitment To Community.

At TMA we believe the work we do creates more than fixed buildings — it creates stronger communities, local jobs, and lasting opportunities for the people we serve.

Why Social Value Matters

Delivering Value Beyond The Contract

Housing associations and local authorities need contractors who contribute to their wider social objectives — not just those who complete the work. TMA has been built around that principle from day one.

From hiring locally and investing in apprenticeships to reducing our environmental footprint and supporting the communities we operate in, social value is embedded into how we work — not added as an afterthought.

Our Social Value Pillars

Six Commitments We Stand Behind

Every contract we take on is an opportunity to deliver value beyond the scope of works. These are the commitments we make to every client.

Local Employment & Skills

We prioritise recruiting from the communities we work in. When new contracts begin, our first call is to local job centres, community groups, and employment schemes.

  • Directly employed local operatives — no agency labour
  • Active engagement with local employment programmes
  • Fair wages above the National Living Wage
  • Guaranteed hours and employment stability

Apprenticeships & Training

We invest in the next generation of tradespeople through apprenticeship programmes, in-house training, and partnerships with local colleges and training providers.

  • Multi-trade apprenticeship programmes
  • NVQ pathways for all core disciplines
  • Mentoring by experienced operatives
  • Partnerships with Midlands training providers

Community Investment

We support the communities our clients serve through charitable giving, volunteering, and contributing to local initiatives that improve quality of life for residents.

  • Annual community fund for resident-led projects
  • Staff volunteering days in contract areas
  • Support for local food banks and charities
  • Resident engagement where requested by clients

Environmental Responsibility

We are committed to reducing the environmental impact of our operations, from the materials we use to the vehicles we drive and the waste we generate on site.

  • Waste segregation and recycling on all sites
  • Responsible sourcing of sustainable materials
  • Fleet transitioning to low-emission vehicles
  • Working towards a carbon reduction plan

Equality, Diversity & Inclusion

We are committed to building an inclusive workplace that reflects the communities we serve — where everyone is treated with dignity, fairness, and respect.

  • Equal opportunities employer — all backgrounds welcome
  • Anti-discrimination policy embedded in all contracts
  • Support for underrepresented groups in the trades
  • Regular EDI training across all staff levels

Health, Safety & Wellbeing

Our operatives are our greatest asset. We invest in their physical and mental wellbeing, maintaining a safety culture that goes far beyond minimum compliance.

  • Mental health first aiders across the business
  • Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for all staff
  • Regular toolbox talks on wellbeing and safety
  • Zero tolerance for unsafe working conditions

How We Deliver

Social Value In Practice

These are the day-to-day practices that underpin our social value commitments on every contract we deliver.

DBS-Checked Operatives

Every TMA operative working in occupied social housing is DBS checked, ensuring residents are safe and clients meet their safeguarding obligations without additional burden.

Local Supply Chain

Where possible we source materials from local and regional suppliers, keeping spend within the communities we serve and reducing transport emissions.

Social Value Reporting

We provide clients with regular social value reporting, quantifying local employment impact, apprenticeship hours, community contributions, and environmental metrics.

Resident-First Approach

Our operatives are trained to interact with residents respectfully and sensitively — especially in supported housing and properties occupied by vulnerable tenants.

Fair Pay Commitment

All TMA employees are paid at or above the Real Living Wage. We do not use zero-hours contracts, and every member of our team receives employment rights from day one.

Modern Slavery Policy

TMA has a zero tolerance approach to modern slavery and human trafficking. We conduct due diligence across our entire supply chain and publish our annual statement in line with legislation.
