Step by Step

From Job Raised to Repair Closed

Seven stages that keep every reactive repair on track and on record.

01 — Job Logging

Raised & Timestamped Immediately

Jobs come in via your housing management system, our helpdesk, or agreed portal integration. Each gets a unique reference and starts the SLA clock at point of receipt.

02 — Triage

Priority Classified Within Minutes

Our coordinators classify every job as Emergency (2hr), Urgent (24hr), or Routine — checking vulnerability flags and safeguarding records before confirming dispatch.

03 — Dispatch

Nearest Skilled Operative Mobilised

Operatives carry comprehensive van stock to maximise first-visit fix rates. Full job info — including any resident notes — is shared before arrival.

04 — On-Site Assessment

ID Presented. Cause Confirmed.

The operative introduces themselves, presents ID, and assesses the defect thoroughly before any work begins. Pre-works photography taken for the job record.

05 — Immediate Works / Make Safe

Fixed on the Spot Where Possible

If a full repair isn't possible on the first visit, a compliant make-safe is implemented immediately — protecting the resident until a follow-on can be booked.

06 — Follow-On Works

Return Booked Before Leaving Site

If a return visit is needed, it's confirmed with the resident before the operative leaves. Parts are pre-ordered and the full scope locked in to guarantee completion on the next visit.

07 — Completion & Reporting

Signed Off. Reported. Closed.

Resident sign-off, post-works photography, and a completion report with SLA performance data — all issued to the client within the agreed reporting window.
